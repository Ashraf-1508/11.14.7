\section*{\textit{\textbf{Question}}}
The motion of a particle executing simple harmonic motion is described by the
displacement function,
$x(t)$ = $A$ $cos$ ($\omega$t +$\phi$).
If the initial $(t = 0)$ position of the particle is 1 cm and its initial velocity is
$\omega$ cm/s, what are its amplitude and initial phase angle ? The angular frequency of the
particle is $\pi s^{-1}$. If instead of the cosine function, we choose the sine function to
describe the SHM : $x$ = $B$ sin ($\omega$t +$\alpha$), what are the amplitude and initial phase of the
particle with the above initial conditions.
